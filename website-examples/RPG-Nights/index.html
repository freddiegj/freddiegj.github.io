Game Etiquette

  • Respect fellow players and the game master.
  • Listen actively and participate collaboratively.
  • Avoid interrupting others while they are speaking.
  • Be mindful of time, ensuring everyone has a chance to contribute.
  • Follow the game rules and guidelines set by the game master.

Things to bring

If this is your first time coming to an RPG night, or you're just getting started with RPGs, then heres a list of things we suggest you bring.

  • Pencils/pens and paper (Not a huge amount, but enough to keep track of things)
  • Dice! (If you don't have any then we can lend you some)
  • If you have any rule books related to the game you want to play, then feel free to bring them along.
  • Water or something to drink

Examples of Games played

Dungeons and Dragons
Fantasy tabletop RPG with immersive storytelling, players create characters and embark on adventures in a magical world, guided by a Dungeon Master.

Pathfinder
Fantasy RPG with deep character customization, players create diverse characters, engage in tactical combat, and embark on epic quests in a rich fantasy world.

Call of Cthulhu
Horror RPG based on Lovecraft's works, players are investigators exploring mysteries, dealing with cosmic horrors, and preserving sanity in a 1920s setting.

Everyone is John
Comedic RPG where players control different personalities within John's mind, competing for control and creating chaotic and humorous situations.
